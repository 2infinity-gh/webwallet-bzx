What is BitcoinZero?

BitcoinZero is a type of digital currency in which encryption techniques are used to regulate the generation of units of currency and verify the transfer of funds, operating independently of a central bank. See https://bitcoinzerox.net for more information.

Privacy

The Bitcoinzero team believes strongly in privacy, not only do we support the use of TOR, the site does not collect and store IP or transaction data via our servers nor do we store your bitcoinzero private key. We do route traffic via an SSL certificate.

Create a New Transaction

Create a transaction, prior to signing and broadcasting to the bitcoinzero network. 

1) Navigate to https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#newTransaction (New > Transaction.)

2) Enter an Address, WIF key, privatekey or Redeem Script and click Load.

Note: Inputs automatically displays a balance.

3) Review Inputs tab to ensure correct data/further information.

4) Enter your Outputs and click Submit. 
Note: Make sure to add a changeaddress if your funds exceed the amount you are sending or else the remaining funds will be lost in fees.

4a) Example of high fees warning. 

5) You are presented with a hex encoded transaction, this can now be verified, signed and then finally broadcast.

Manual

Create a MultiSig Address

Multisignature (multisig) refers to requiring more than one (private) key to authorize a Bitcoin transaction. It is generally used to divide up responsibility for possession of bitcoins.

Use example: Bob is purchasing an item from Lucy, Bob wants to ensure Lucy delivers the item before Bobs payment is released to Lucy.  A trusted third party (the escrow agent) is introduced, his name is John.   
A multisig wallet is created by Bob, configured so that it requires two signatures (out of three) to release the payment to Lucys wallet. 
Bob sends the required payment amount to the multisig wallet address, of which all parties can view the balance publicly.   
Lucy sends the item to Bob and upon delivery Bob sends John a picture of the item in his possession. At this stage both Bob and John can sign the transaction and release payment to Lucy.   If however the conditions of the trade are not met, John simply provides arbitration to resolve the disagreement and John can choose either Bob or Lucy to sign the transaction with (payment vs refund.)

1) Navigate to https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#newMultiSig (New > MultiSig Address) to create a MultiSig address.

2) Choose how many participants are required (this example uses three) by adding and/or removing, using the relative buttons (+/-) to the right of the public key text fields. 

2a) Insert the public keys of the participants addresses (you/participants can create wallets at https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#newAddress)

2c) Select the amount of signatures required to release the coins.

2d) Once you have confirmed the correct input, click Submit.

3) You will now be presented with a payment Address*, Redeem Script** and Shareable URL***. 

*Contributors should send the required payment amount to this address.
**All participants should be provided with the redeem script, prior to payments being made.
***This URL will direct participants to a read-only page with the information generated by you (see image B). 

Image B

Note: You can manually verify the redeem script by entering it at https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#verify (see Image B above.)

 

QUICK HINT: How to spend from a MultiSig Address

In this example, two of three private keys/participants are required to sign the redeem script.

One of three participants must now generate a new transaction using the redeem script created in step 1.

Once a transaction has been created, the same participant must sign the transaction using their private key. 

Once signed, the participant will share the signed hex transaction to another participant required to sign.

The next participant will then sign the transaction using their own private key.

Finally, once all signature conditions are met, the final output can be broadcast to the network.

Manual

Verify

Verify raw transactions, redeem scripts, pubkeys, hd addresses or WIF keys to ensure they are correct. 

Use example: Bob has been provided a redeem script as part of a MultiSig transaction, he is the buyer in this example and has been asked to fund a bitcoinzero address.  Bob can verify the redeem script to ensure legitimacy as per the trade agreement. 
Use example 2: Bob wants to verify a generated transaction prior to broadcasting, to ensure it is correct.

1) Navigate to https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#verify (Verify.)


2) Input a raw transaction, redeem script, pubkey, hd address or WIF key in to the text field, then click Submit.

3) The verified output is displayed. 

Manual

Sign a Transaction

You will need to sign a transaction before you can broadcast the transaction to the Bitcoinzero network.

1) Navigate to https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#sign(Sign.)


2) Enter the Private Key required to sign the transaction, the transaction hex and click Submit. 

3) You are presented with a signed hex encoded transaction as confirmation. 

Manual

Broadcast a Transaction

You will need to broadcast a transaction to the Bitcoinzero network, this is the final step in most cases. 

1) Navigate to https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#broadcast (Broadcast.)


2) Enter your hex encoded bitcoin transaction and click Submit.

3) You are presented with a txid: as confirmation. 

3.a) You can use the txid to confirm your transaction (normally within minutes.)

Manual

How to use Wallet

This page can generate a wallet within your web browser, using JavaScript. You do not need to be connected to the internet to generate/access a wallet, but you will not see an accurate balance without connection. 

1) Navigate to https://webwallet.bitcoinzerox.net/#wallet (Wallet.)


2) Enter an email address and password then click Submit.

3) You are now logged in to a wallet. 

 

4) You can Spend (send) bitcoinzero directly from your wallet (internet connection required.)

Fill out the Spend requirements; Address & Amount (you can add multiple lines +) and finally the Transaction Fee.

 

4a) Click Send to present a confirmation prompt. 

4b) After clicking Send within the confirmation prompt, the transaction has been broadcast to the bitcoinzero network, a txid is presented. 

4c) Example of incorrect amounts set. (Remember to calculate fee cost from balance.)

5) View your wallet keys. 

Note: you can reveal your private key by clicking Show.

6) Be sure to Logout of your wallet when you are finished. 

This page uses javascript to generate your addresses and sign your transactions within your browser, this means we never receive your private keys, this can be independently verified by reviewing the source code on github

Always consider using this page offline.
